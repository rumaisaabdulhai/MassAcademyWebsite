Computer Science is taught by Mrs. Taricco. In Mass Academy, few students who have prior computer science experience embark on an independent project. For my project, I chose to explore the world of machine learning. I performed Image Classification where I evaluated two Machine Learning techniques, namely the K-Nearest Neighbor (KNN) Classifier and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). I worked on it throughout B term and C Term. In D term, I embarked on an Apps For Good Project with 2 other MAMS students to help high school students find community service opportunities.

Image Classification Project

I have been fascinated with how image classification works in the field of computer vision as it has various applications in medicine, security, and self-driving cars. For example, doctors use image classification in CT scans to identify cancers and tumors. Law enforcement can use image classification to identify suspects. The most interesting application, however, is in autonomous vehicles such as self-driving cars, where pedestrians, objects, or animals must be identified quickly in time for the car to perform obstacle avoidance. My goal is that if an unclassified image is fed into my program, it will identify the object by classifying it into one of several categories.

Apps For Good Project

During D Term, my team and I developed an Android Studio Application in Java called HelpOut to help high school students search for volunteer opportunities that interest them. You can find more information on our project below.

The Problem

Many students with mandatory community service requirements or students looking to give back to their community struggle to find volunteer opportunities that interest them. There are 16% of potential volunteers who do not volunteer due to a lack of nearby volunteer opportunities (Kotecha). Another common reason why people do not volunteer is a lack of interesting volunteer opportunities (Youtopoulus). Therefore, there is a need to provide a platform where potential volunteers can be fully aware of interesting volunteer opportunities nearby and organizations can market their opportunities. Moreover, many nonprofits and other organizations often struggle to pick up interested volunteers. We believe that this arises due to the lack of a common platform between volunteers and organizations. Indeed, the problem is the accessibility of community service opportunities for both people looking to volunteer and organizations that could use potential volunteers.

Target Audience

HelpOut is targeted towards students who want to volunteer in their communities, but cannot find the right opportunity for them. The app is also aimed at organizations looking for volunteers for events, fundraisers, and as long-term helpers. HelpOut would allow not only for students to find a place to enjoy volunteering, but also allow organizations to gain volunteers that are eager to work in a certain field.

Features of Our App

The first main feature of the app is that students may sort opportunities by location and by name. The second is that they can save opportunities to view later by marking it as favorite. First, I used Web Scraping to obtain the title, description, location, contact information, and more for about 135 volunteer opportunities and created a custom database. I stored the database on Firebase, a service by Google for storing data on the cloud. The Android Application reads and displays the data from Firebase. You can view the Java code here, and the Web Scraping code here.

The video below shows a demo of the application. When the user opens the app for the first time, they are brought to a welcome screen that walks them through the app's features. They are then prompted to create an account or sign in. Any opportunities that they favorite or unfavorite will be updated and saved on the cloud and accessible from any device as long as they can sign in.
