MIT Lemelson InvenTeams Grant

In the summer of 2020, I led a project with three classmates to help high school students to search, find, and apply to volunteer opportunities, summer programs, and internships. High school students currently have to spend hours searching through outdated content on numerous websites to find opportunities at hospitals, universities, or companies. Our proposed solution is HelpOut: a mobile application on iOS and Android platforms that would serve as a single destination for high schoolers to effectively search, filter, and directly apply for programs they are passionate about. We developed many of the Android mobile app features over the summer, conducted a design study to assess the need for such a platform, wrote a grant proposal, and reached out to high schools and organizations in Massachusetts for support. Our team was selected to be a finalist for the grant. We hope to continue the project during senior year. The presentation below gives an overview of the project and also features some demos.

Beaver Works Aerial Vehicle Racing

In the summer of 2019, I attended the Beaver Works Program at MIT for 4 weeks as a sophomore. I learned how to pilot and program a quadcopter using Python, the Robot Operating System (ROS), and openCV. Every day we would attend seminars from industry leaders including Lincoln Labs and BAE Systems. My experience at Beaver Works inspired me to pursue my autonomous drone application to fight mass shootings.

During the first week, we practiced safety protocol and flying the Intel quadcopter. For the second week, we competed in small challenges such as programming the quadcopter to fly in certain configurations and avoid AR-tagged obstacles. We were even able to trace the shape of Beaver mascot with a light mounted on the quadcopter and a long exposure camera (left).

For the third week, we learned how to use openCV for computer vision so we could get our drone to follow an LED-illuminated path. Finally, during the fourth week, we prepared for the final racing competition and programmed our quadcopter to follow an LED-illuminated path while avoiding AR-tagged obstacles. The left video is a snippet of our drone navigating the obstacle course autonomously. We also made a final presentation that gives an overview of our code.

We modeled population density, product density, obstruction of view from cashiers, and number of customers to develop a scoring system to predict the total number of damaged products in a flash sale.
