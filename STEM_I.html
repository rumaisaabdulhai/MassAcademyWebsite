Simulation of an Indoor Autonomous Drone for Assisting Police in Mass Shootings

The STEM I and II Courses are taught by Dr. Crowthers at Mass Academy. In STEM I, Mass Academy juniors are required to conduct a 5-month long science or engineering project. In A Term, we brainstormed ideas for our STEM Project. In B Term, we conducted a Literature Review on relevant literature in our project's field. During December Fair, we presented our research and preliminary results on our project to advisors and Mass Academy seniors. We also have a February Fair where we present our STEM Project to the public. I am working on an engineering project using autonomous drones to assist law enforcement during mass shootings and save precious lives.

RRT Path Planning Implementation

Path planning algorithms are necessary to determine an optimal path from a chosen source point to a destination point and are used extensively in the fields of robotics. The Rapidly Exploring Random Tree (RRT) Algorithm caught my eye as it can determine a possible path in a very short amount of time. I used path planning after I mapped the indoor environment so my drone could navigate from one location to another. I coded the RRT Algorithm in a simple 2D Python Simulator called Pygame.
