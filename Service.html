Lego Summer Robotics Camp

Last summer in 2019, I volunteered at my local mosque, the Worcester Islamic Center, and taught students from grades 4-8 about building and programming robots with Lego Mindstorms. My tasks included helping the students connect the different parts such as actuators and sensors, and guiding students with their programs using block coding. During my time, I worked closely with two female students in the 3rd and 4th grade. Seeing their excited and proud faces when they finally got their robot to follow a black line was really inspiring for me. Even though it was a bit difficult trying to teach them when they had limited knowledge of concepts such as speed, I had a lot of fun and hoped they learned a lot too.

Helping Hand

Since 2018, I have volunteered with Helping Hand (HHRD). HHRD is a humanitarian relief organization that works to support refugee families in countries such as Jordan, Lebanon, Somalia, and Pakistan. My tasks included reaching out to past donors to donate for a Water and Sanitation Hygiene (WASH) fundraiser to support the building of water and sanitation facilities for Syrian families in Jordan and Lebanon. I was one of the youth speakers in the fundraiser. We were able to raise $40,000 dollars in total. Working with Helping Hand has been a really meaningful experience for me. I know the excellent work that they are doing to provide disaster stricken communities with essentials such as food, shelter, and water and want to involve myself more directly by giving back in whatever way I can.
